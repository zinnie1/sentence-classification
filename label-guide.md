# Sentence Classification Label Guide

## Purpose

This document defines the labeling standards used throughout this repository. The goal is to ensure consistent, accurate, and objective sentence classification.

---

# 1. Sentiment Classification

Determine the emotional tone of a sentence.

## Positive

The sentence expresses satisfaction, happiness, appreciation, optimism, or praise.

Examples:

- The customer service was excellent.
- I really enjoyed using this application.
- Everything worked perfectly.

---

## Neutral

The sentence presents factual information without expressing emotion.

Examples:

- The meeting begins at 9 AM.
- Your order has been shipped.
- The document contains five sections.

---

## Negative

The sentence expresses dissatisfaction, frustration, disappointment, or criticism.

Examples:

- The application keeps crashing.
- I am disappointed with the service.
- The package arrived damaged.

---

# 2. Topic Classification

Assign each sentence to the most appropriate subject.

Available topics include:

- Technology
- Finance
- Healthcare
- Education
- Sports
- Entertainment
- Customer Support
- Travel
- Business

Choose the category that best represents the main subject of the sentence.

---

# 3. Intent Classification

Determine what the speaker wants to accomplish.

Possible intents include:

- Refund Request
- Order Tracking
- Technical Support
- Product Inquiry
- Cancellation Request
- Complaint
- Feedback
- General Inquiry

Always classify based on the speaker's primary intention.

---

# Annotation Principles

Every annotation should follow these principles:

- Read the full sentence carefully.
- Consider the surrounding context when available.
- Apply labels consistently.
- Avoid personal opinions.
- Choose the single best label.
- Maintain objectivity.
- Document uncertain cases for review.

---

# Quality Assurance

Annotations are reviewed using the following criteria:

- Accuracy
- Consistency
- Completeness
- Context awareness
- Label correctness

---

This project demonstrates practical NLP annotation skills commonly used in AI model development, machine learning datasets, and Large Language Model (LLM) evaluation.
