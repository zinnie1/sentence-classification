# Intent Classification Examples

## Objective

Identify the primary intent behind each user statement.

### Available Intent Labels

- Product Inquiry
- Order Tracking
- Refund Request
- Technical Support
- Account Management
- Complaint
- Feedback
- General Inquiry

---

## Example 1

**Sentence**

> Where is my order? It was supposed to arrive yesterday.

**Intent**

Order Tracking

**Reason**

The customer wants to know the current delivery status.

---

## Example 2

**Sentence**

> I would like a refund because the item arrived damaged.

**Intent**

Refund Request

**Reason**

The user is requesting reimbursement for a defective product.

---

## Example 3

**Sentence**

> I can't log into my account even after resetting my password.

**Intent**

Technical Support

**Reason**

The user needs assistance resolving a technical issue.

---

## Example 4

**Sentence**

> Does this laptop come with a one-year warranty?

**Intent**

Product Inquiry

**Reason**

The customer is asking for product information before purchasing.

---

## Example 5

**Sentence**

> Please delete my account and remove all my personal information.

**Intent**

Account Management

**Reason**

The request concerns account settings and user data.

---

## Example 6

**Sentence**

> Your mobile app is slow and crashes frequently.

**Intent**

Complaint

**Reason**

The user is reporting dissatisfaction with the application's performance.

---

## Example 7

**Sentence**

> I really like the new interface. It's much easier to use.

**Intent**

Feedback

**Reason**

The customer is sharing positive feedback about the product.

---

## Example 8

**Sentence**

> What are your customer service hours?

**Intent**

General Inquiry

**Reason**

The user is requesting general information.

---

## Annotation Notes

When labeling intent:

- Focus on the user's primary goal.
- Ignore emotional tone unless it changes the intent.
- Select only one intent label.
- Apply labels consistently across similar requests.

---

## Summary

This exercise demonstrates the ability to identify user intent accurately, a critical skill for conversational AI, virtual assistants, customer support systems, and large language model (LLM) training datasets.
