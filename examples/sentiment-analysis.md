# Sentiment Analysis Examples

## Objective

Classify each sentence according to its emotional sentiment.

Available labels:

- Positive
- Neutral
- Negative

---

## Example 1

Sentence:

> I absolutely love the new update. Everything works much faster now.

Label:

**Positive**

Reason:

The sentence expresses satisfaction and appreciation.

---

## Example 2

Sentence:

> Your order has been shipped and will arrive tomorrow.

Label:

**Neutral**

Reason:

The sentence simply provides information without emotion.

---

## Example 3

Sentence:

> The application crashes every time I try to log in.

Label:

**Negative**

Reason:

The speaker expresses frustration with the application's performance.

---

## Example 4

Sentence:

> Customer support solved my problem within minutes.

Label:

**Positive**

Reason:

The sentence communicates a successful and satisfying experience.

---

## Example 5

Sentence:

> The conference starts at 10:00 AM tomorrow.

Label:

**Neutral**

Reason:

This is factual information without emotional content.

---

## Example 6

Sentence:

> I regret purchasing this product because it stopped working after one day.

Label:

**Negative**

Reason:

The sentence clearly expresses disappointment.

---

## Summary

This exercise demonstrates the ability to distinguish between positive, neutral, and negative sentiment while maintaining consistent annotation standards.
